Beef Stew

Home

A hearty beef stew with vegetables and herbs, perfect for a cozy meal.

Recipe by Gerren Ballard

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs beef chuck, cut into 1-inch cubes
  • 4 cups beef broth
  • 3 carrots, sliced
  • 2 potatoes, diced
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tbsp tomato paste
  • 1 tsp thyme
  • 1 bay leaf
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 cup red wine (optional)
  • Fresh parsley for garnish

Instructions

  1. In a large pot, heat olive oil over medium-high heat. Add beef and brown on all sides.
  2. Add onion and garlic, saut√© until softened.
  3. Stir in tomato paste, thyme, bay leaf, salt, and pepper.
  4. Add carrots, potatoes, and beef broth. If using red wine, add it now.
  5. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to low. Cover and simmer for 1.5 to 2 hours or until beef is tender.
  6. Remove bay leaf before serving. Garnish with fresh parsley.
  7. Serve hot with crusty bread or over rice.

Notes

For a thicker stew, you can mix 2 tablespoons of cornstarch with 2 tablespoons of water and stir it into the stew during the last 10 minutes of cooking.

Feel free to add other vegetables like peas or green beans for extra flavor and nutrition.

Serving Suggestions

This beef stew pairs well with a side of crusty bread or over a bed of rice. You can also serve it with a fresh green salad for a complete meal.

Storage Instructions

Store any leftovers in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. Reheat on the stove or in the microwave before serving.

Freezing Instructions

This beef stew can be frozen for up to 3 months. Allow it to cool completely before transferring it to a freezer-safe container. Thaw in the refrigerator overnight before reheating.

Variations

You can customize this beef stew by adding different vegetables or spices. Try adding mushrooms, bell peppers, or even a splash of Worcestershire sauce for extra flavor.

Tips

For the best flavor, use a good quality beef broth and let the stew simmer for a longer time. The longer it cooks, the more tender the beef will become.
