Stuffed Meatballs

Delicious meatballs stuffed with gooey mozzarella cheese and simmered in marinara sauce.

Recipe by Gerren Ballard

Ingredients

  • 1 lb ground beef
  • 1/2 cup breadcrumbs
  • 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1 egg
  • 1/2 cup marinara sauce
  • 4 oz mozzarella cheese, cut into small cubes
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh parsley
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Olive oil for frying

Instructions

  1. In a large bowl, combine ground beef, breadcrumbs, Parmesan cheese, egg, parsley, salt, and pepper.
  2. Form the meat mixture into small balls and press a cube of mozzarella cheese into the center of each ball.
  3. Heat olive oil in a skillet over medium heat. Fry meatballs until browned on all sides.
  4. Transfer meatballs to a pot and cover with marinara sauce.
  5. Simmer over low heat for 20-25 minutes, or until meatballs are cooked through.
  6. Serve hot with pasta or crusty bread.

Notes

You can use ground turkey or chicken instead of beef for a lighter version of this recipe.

Feel free to add other seasonings like garlic powder, onion powder, or Italian seasoning for extra flavor.

Serving Suggestions

These stuffed meatballs are delicious served over spaghetti or with a side of garlic bread. You can also enjoy them on their own as an appetizer.

Storage Instructions

Store any leftovers in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. Reheat on the stove or in the microwave before serving.

Freezing Instructions

These meatballs can be frozen for up to 3 months. Allow them to cool completely before transferring them to a freezer-safe container. Thaw in the refrigerator overnight before reheating.

Variations

You can customize these meatballs by using different types of cheese or adding other fillings like chopped spinach or sun-dried tomatoes.

Tips

Make sure to seal the mozzarella cheese completely inside the meatball to prevent it from oozing out during cooking.

For extra flavor, brown the meatballs in the skillet before simmering them in the marinara sauce.

Use a cookie scoop or spoon to portion out the meat mixture evenly for uniform meatballs.
