Chicken Parmesan

Delicious breaded chicken cutlets topped with marinara sauce and melted cheese.

Recipe by Gerren Ballard

Ingredients

  • 4 boneless, skinless chicken breasts
  • 1 cup breadcrumbs
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1 cup marinara sauce
  • 1 cup shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 2 eggs, beaten
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Olive oil for frying
  • Fresh basil for garnish

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Season chicken breasts with salt and pepper.
  3. Dip each chicken breast in beaten eggs, then coat with a mixture of breadcrumbs and Parmesan cheese.
  4. Heat olive oil in a skillet over medium heat. Fry chicken until golden brown on both sides.
  5. Transfer chicken to a baking dish. Top each piece with marinara sauce and mozzarella cheese.
  6. Bake in the preheated oven for 20-25 minutes, or until cheese is melted and bubbly.
  7. Garnish with fresh basil before serving.
  8. Serve with pasta or a side salad.

Notes

You can use store-bought marinara sauce or make your own. For a healthier version, bake the chicken instead of frying.

Serving Suggestions

This chicken parmesan pairs well with spaghetti, garlic bread, or a fresh green salad. You can also serve it with steamed vegetables for a lighter meal.

Storage Instructions

Store any leftovers in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. Reheat in the oven or microwave before serving.

Freezing Instructions

This chicken parmesan can be frozen for up to 3 months. Allow it to cool completely before transferring it to a freezer-safe container. Thaw in the refrigerator overnight before reheating.

Variations

You can customize this chicken parmesan by adding different cheeses or spices. Try using provolone cheese instead of mozzarella, or add Italian seasoning to the breadcrumb mixture for extra flavor.

Tips

For extra crispy chicken, let the breaded chicken rest for 10-15 minutes before frying. This helps the coating adhere better.

Use a meat mallet to pound the chicken breasts to an even thickness for more even cooking.
